## Question 2: How can you use B = MAP to make making new habits easier? What are M, A and P.

- B = MAP means Behavior = Motivation + Ability + Prompt.  
- **M (Motivation):** How much you want to do it.  
- **A (Ability):** How easy it is to do.  
- **P (Prompt):** The trigger or reminder.  
- All three together make building new habits easier.  

---

## Question 3: Why it is important to "Shine" or Celebrate after each successful completion of habit?

- Celebrating makes you feel good about the habit.  
- Your brain remembers the positive feeling and repeats it.  
- Whatever you celebrate becomes stronger and turns into a habit faster.

## Question 5: What is the book's perspective about Identity?

- Habits are linked to identity.  
- Instead of saying “I want to run,” say “I am a runner.”  
- When habits become part of who you are, they last longer.

## Question 6: Write about the book's perspective on how to make a habit easier to do.

- Reduce friction and make the habit simple.  
- Prepare your environment in advance.  
- Example: keep water nearby to drink more or keep shoes ready for exercise.  

---

## Question 7: Write about the book's perspective on how to make a habit harder to do.

- Increase friction and make the habit inconvenient.  
- Add steps between you and the bad habit.  
- Example: remove distracting apps or keep junk food far away.

## Question 8: Pick one habit that you would like to do more of? What are the steps that you can take to make it make the cue obvious or the habit more attractive or easy and or response satisfying?

- Habit: Reading books daily.  
- Steps:  
  - Place a book on my desk so it is visible (cue obvious).  
  - Choose topics I enjoy (make it attractive).  
  - Start with 2 pages a day (make it easy).  
  - Celebrate after finishing (make it satisfying).  

---

## Question 9: Pick one habit that you would like to eliminate or do less of? What are the steps that you can take to make it make the cue invisible or the process unattractive or hard or the response unsatisfying?

- Habit: Using phone too much at night.  
- Steps:  
  - Keep phone in another room before sleeping (cue invisible).  
  - Turn off notifications and remove distracting apps (make it unattractive).  
  - Use a normal alarm clock instead of phone (make it hard).  
  - Remind myself that scrolling late makes me tired (make it unsatisfying).
